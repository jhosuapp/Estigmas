Schizophrenia is not being crazy

Schizophrenia is a serious mental illness that affects more than 21 million people around the world and is the most distressing and disabling mental disorder in existence, according to the World Health Organization.

Schizophrenia is a severe mental disorder that affects some brain functions such as thinking, perception, emotions, and behavior. It usually manifests itself in people in their late teens, as stated by Janssen. It is a chronic disease that currently has no cure and that affects all aspects of the daily life of those who suffer from it.

It is said that schizophrenic patients tend to lose touch with reality due to hallucinations*, delusions*, disordered thoughts, and peculiar ways of speaking or behaving. ways of speaking or behaving.

This is probably the most distressing and disabling mental disorder of all because its symptoms affect a person's ability to relate to others and remove them from the world around them. However, it is a disease treatable with pharmacotherapy and other psychosocial support.

f you suffer from schizophrenia or think you have any of the symptoms, you are in time to receive treatment, consult a health professional for more information.

Where does schizophrenia come from?

Diagnosing a schizophrenia condition is not an easy task.

Cause

An individual factor has not yet been identified as the sole cause of schizophrenia. It is considered that this can be caused by the interaction between genetics and a series of external factors such as, for example, drug and alcohol consumption, living in poverty or in stressful environments and exposure to viruses or prenatal nutrition problems, as stated by Janssen.

In the last decade, evidence of structural abnormalities has been shown in the brains of patients with schizophrenia. A study published by the American Journal of Pshychiatry revealed that after having an episode of psychosis, patients had less brain tissue than healthy people, limiting communication between neurons. neurons.

Diagnosis

Diagnosing schizophrenia is not an easy job and that is why misdiagnoses occur. Before drawing conclusions, other possible causes of the symptoms, such as drug use, epilepsy* or brain tumors, must be eliminated. For the diagnosis of a schizophrenic condition, it is necessary that the symptoms have been present for at least 6 months and that there have been several psychotic episodes* with specific characteristics.

Currently, there is no analytical test that can confirm a condition of schizophrenia. The diagnosis of this disease is based on the psychiatrist's clinical interviews with the patient and with the family and some physical tests, such as a brain scan and a psychosocial evaluation.

Discover the symptoms

Schizophrenia is a type of mental illness that generates distortions in an individual's thinking, emotions, perceptions, language, consciousness, and behavior.

Some of the symptoms are positive r new to the individual as a result of the mental disorder; others are negative, that is to say, they indicate an impoverishment in the patient's personality and mood; and others are cognitive, which means that they constitute a decrease in attention, memory and other functions.

Hallucinations: (Positive)

Seeing, hearing or feeling something that is not really there. They can occur in any of the five senses.

Delusions: (Positive)

Strongly held irrational and false beliefs produced by the inability to separate real from unreal experiences.

Disorderly thoughts (Positive)

Making disorganized associations. Draw conclusions that are not based on reality. Lack of logical reasoning.

Agitation (Positive)

Tense and irritated state.

Lack of motivation (Negative)

Spending a lot of time in bed with no motivation to do.

Social withdrawal (Negative)

Giving the impression that the affected persons prefer to be alone and do not want to see other people.

Apathy (Negative)

Feelings of emptiness. Inability to follow through with plans.

Emotional numbness (Negative)

Lack of normal signs of emotion.

Source: Janssen.

Types of schizophrenia

In order to facilitate the diagnosis of schizophrenia, psychiatrists have classified several types, based on the behaviors, symptoms and feelings of those who suffer from it.

Undifferentiated Type

Because the symptoms of schizophrenia vary and get mixed a lot, depending on the person who contracts the disease, there are some that cannot be classified within those mentioned.

Disorganized or hebephric type

Presents concentration problems, confusion, strange ideas, delusions, incoherent or difficult to understand speech. In addition, they do not show emotion, or respond inappropriately to different stimuli.

Catatonic type

Little reaction to stimuli, immobility, rigid or unusual postures, and exalted physical activity for no apparent reason.

Paranoid type

They have extreme distrust, delusions and hallucinations with persecution, anxiety, anger, jealousy, and irrational violence in various occasions.

Residual type

The patient has had at least one episode of schizophrenia, but has no delusions, hallucinations, or disorganized speech or behavior. Negative symptoms such as lack of interest and emotional and social isolation remain.

Depression is not being boring

Depression is a mental illness that affects more than 300 million people and is the #1 cause of disability worldwide, according to the World Health Organization.

It is determined by multiple factors that affect a person in different aspects of his or her life, from emotional stability to physical appearance.

It is a common mental disorder characterized by a feeling of sadness that interferes with the development of usual activities such as work or social relationships.

If you suffer from depression or think you have any of the symptoms, you are in time to receive treatment, consult a health professional for more information.

Where does depression come from?

Depression is the result of complex interactions between social, psychological, and biological factors.

Biological origin:

The biological origin of depression remains a mystery, however, one of the most popular theories is that is caused by alterations in the brain neurotransmitters*, such as serotonin and noradrenaline. These neurotransmitters are associated with feelings of happiness and wellbeing, as stated by Janssen.

Other factors:

There are other social and psychological factors that tend to make people more prone to depression. For example, people who have gone through adverse life circumstances, such as the death of someone close to them, unemployment, or other psychological trauma*, so states the WHO.

Discover the symptoms

People suffering from depression experience multiple symptoms, such as low mood or loss of interest in certain activities. To be diagnosed as depression, the person must have 5 or more symptoms for a period of at least 2 weeks.

Anxiety

This is the condition of a person experiencing shock, uneasiness, nervousness or worry.

Psychomotor agitation

It is the extreme form of restlessness or hyperactivity, characterized by increased movements not directed to the achievement of a specific goal.

Difficulty concentrating

Also to remember details or make decisions.

Unexplained feelings of guilt

These people may feel helpless or worthless.

Persistent sadness or depressive moods

This is one of the most representative symptoms of depression and usually interferes with daily activities.

Significant change in appetite and sleep disturbances

Eating more or less than usual or having difficulty falling asleep or waking up (insomnia or hypersomnia)

Low energy or fatigue

It is the feeling of extreme tiredness, exhaustion or weakness that can make everyday tasks more difficult.

Loss of interest

Decreased pleasure in activities normally enjoyed.

Suicidal thoughts or acts

Thinking about suicide or hurting oneself.

Types of depression

Major depressive disorder

Major depressive disorder is the most recurrent condition among all types of depression.

It is a very common mood disorder around the world, while still being considered a serious and serious illness. It is characterized by the recurrent occurrence of a major depressive episode, lasting at least 2 weeks and presenting changes in affect and cognition.

Patients with MDD are 20 times more at risk of suicide than the rest of the population, according to Janssen.

Recurrent depressive disorder (dysthymia)

It is a disorder that lasts at least 2 years. Diagnosed persons may suffer major depressive episodes, but also present stages in which symptoms are less severe.

Treatment-resistant depression

It occurs in patients with major depressive disorder when they have not responded to adequate doses of two different antidepressants given for a sufficient period of time, usually six weeks.

Postpartum depression

Women with this disorder suffer from major depression during pregnancy or after childbirth. It is characterized by the onset of feelings of extreme sadness, anxiety and exhaustion that interfere with mothers' daily activities and the care of their babies.

Psychotic depression

This form of depression occurs when the person suffers from severe depressive episodes, but, in addition, presents some form of psychosis, such as the appearance of false beliefs (delusions) or seeing things that others cannot see (hallucinations).

Bipolar depressive disorder

It is different from depression, but it is part of this list because a person suffering from this disorder experiences episodes of very low mood, which is categorized as major depression.

Source of types of depression: National Institute of Mental Health

Treatment Resistant Depression

One in three patients with MDD are treatment resistant. Depression referred to as Treatment-resistant depression or DRT occurs in patients when standard medications often provide minimal or no relief, as their bodies do not respond to adequate doses given for sufficient periods of time (6 weeks), as stated by Janssen.

The above presents a concerning burden for people suffering from this type of depression who require an urgent solution to their illness.

There is hope for DRT patients

Treatments available to treat depressive symptoms it takes between 4 and 6 weeks to take full effect.

This is a need that has existed for 30 years, according to Janssen.

There are new treatment delivery models and innovative formulations that target different pathways in the brain and reduce depressive symptoms in as little as 24 hours.

This holds out a great hope for all patients with treatment-resistant depression, who had very few options for the control and relief of their symptomatology.
